About Me

About my research

My research focuses on American electoral politics and statistical research methods.

I am interested in the performance and functioning of elections: how well elections represent citizen views, how elections are administered, how citizens experience the task of voting. I have studied these topics with historical election returns, online experiments, targeted surveys, voting and registration records, and polling place reports. I am a student affiliate of the Elections Research Center at UW–Madison.

My publications and current projects deal with voter identification requirements in the state of Wisconsin, the gender gap and other group dynamics in electoral coalitions, voters’ perceptions of uncertainty in election polls and forecasts, and Bayesian causal inference.

My methodological interests are focused on the development and application of Bayesian methods for generative modeling, measurement, and causal inference. I am also concerned with meta-research problems related to statistical and scientific inference, transparency and reproducibility, publication bias, and computing practices for social science research. Accordingly, my work is open-source.

My dissertation develops new Bayesian methods to study ideological representation in congressional primary elections. These innovations include

  1. Extensions of hierarchical item response models to estimate the aggregate policy preferences of partisan constituents
  2. Theoretical framework and practical guidance for Bayesian causal models, which are nearly absent from academic politics research.
  3. Flexible machine learning methods for Bayesian causal modeling.

About my teaching

My teaching focuses on the mathematical, statistical, and software foundations for social science research. I h ave taught courses and workshops focused on causal inference, predictive modeling, statistical computing, survey science, voting and public opinion, and U.S. campaign finance.

I have been recognized with the Honored Instructor award at UW–Madison.
