A Visualization of Partial Effects in Multiple Regression

Written by Mike DeCrescenzo on October 19, 2018 in Methods

Background

A few days ago, Andrew Heiss was looking for a way to visualize multiple regression with an emphasis on one predictor, without 3(+)-dimensional plots.

I'm not a fan of 3D scatterplots to show effect of multiple independent variables on y (especially when adding more x variables). What's the best way to visually show students the idea of "controlling for" other variables?

Intuition

\begin{align} y{i} &= \alpha + \beta\left(\mathrm{Humidity}{i}\right) + \sum{j}\gamma{j}x{ji} + \varepsilon{i} \end{align}

A function of humidity, plus other covariates $j \in {1…J}$ and their associated coefficients $\gamma_{j}$
